# PAYROLL — Brand Note

**Title:** PAYROLL
**Subtitle:** A Private Military Sim
**Genre:** Management sim / autobattler · near-future cyberpunk · up to battalion scale
**Platforms:** Steam demo first, mobile-native follow-up
**Hook:** *You run the company. The company runs the war.*

## The thesis in one line
Every screen is a tactical console that a PMC operations officer would actually use. The game's answer to every design question is: *what would the company's internal tool look like?*

## Tonal register
Cold. Professional. Corporate violence with a paper trail. Not edgy, not snarky, not grim-for-grim's-sake. The humor is dry and legal-adjacent (it's called PAYROLL because you literally pay people to do this). Think the tonal space between **Michael Mann**, **Zero Dark Thirty**, and **Cyberpunk 2077's corpo route** — never **Borderlands**, never **Fortnite**, never winking.

## Voice & vocabulary
- Callsigns in double quotes: `"HOLST"`, `"VANCE"`
- Operators are *operators*, not heroes or mercs or units in UI copy
- Squads are *elements* in briefing context, *squads* in armory context
- Contracts have codes (`OP-CLEARWATER`), clients (`MERIDIAN RISK`), and categories (`ASSET RECOVERY`, `DENIAL`, `DEFENSIVE ESCORT`)
- Money is `$` with comma separators, or `cr` (credits) in sci-fi framing contexts — pick one per direction and stay consistent
- Timestamps: 24h, `14:32:08`
- Outcomes are stated in after-action language: `MISSION COMPLETE`, `CONTRACT FORFEIT`, `ASSET COMPROMISED`

## Visual DNA
Three directions explored in Round 1; one (or a mix) chosen before rollout.
- **A · BLACKSITE** — amber-CRT terminal. Black + amber + cyan. Scanlines in combat only. Mono everywhere.
- **B · NEON WIRE** — hex-clipped panels, deep navy, triple-accent (cyan/amber/magenta), Chakra Petch display.
- **C · DOSSIER** — paper + chassis, stamps, olive drab, redactions, typewriter.

## Non-negotiables across all directions
- The combat view is **top-down painterly**, Firefight-DNA. Tracers, muzzle flash, volumetric smoke, LOS fog, silhouette units with facing ticks. Never a schematic grid unless toggled by the player.
- The armory is a **paperdoll / MWO mechlab**. 11 body zones, drag-to-zone, live weight bar, delta previews on hover.
- No emoji. Icons are monochrome line-SVG.
- No decorative color. Every accent color encodes state.
- Mono fonts always use tabular figures; columns of numbers align perfectly.
- Type hierarchy is enforced. One display face, one body face, one mono face. Never more.

## Company / world framing (seed fiction, refine later)
- You own a mid-tier PMC in the 2030s–2040s, operating out of a megacity arcology.
- Clients are corporate, criminal, municipal. Contracts vary: extraction, denial, escort, sweep, recovery.
- Your roster scales from a 4-op team to a full battalion across a campaign.
- The UI is your internal ops console — a bespoke piece of software the company licenses.
- Name-drops in placeholder content should reinforce the fiction: `MERIDIAN RISK`, `KESSLER HOLDINGS`, `DAIGO COMBINE`, `NORTH SIXTH PRECINCT`, `OP-CLEARWATER`, `OP-BLACKLINE`, `MAP: YARD-07 · WAREHOUSE DISTRICT`.

## What to avoid
- Anything that looks like a Unity asset store HUD
- Anything Orbitron / Exo / Eurostile / Starcraft-futurist
- Any emoji, any Unicode glyph decoration
- Centered-button main menus
- Admin-panel dashboards with left rails and giant headings (Clutch Legend is the enemy)
- Decorative glows, fake scanlines on non-combat screens (unless the direction explicitly commits to it)
- "Sci-fi chrome" — beveled buttons, metal textures, drop shadows on panels
